Question 1#

To calculate the running total, we can start with T=0 and add the interest in each step. It is important that the variables \(T,M\) are initialised outside the loop so that they are not reset each time!

apr=0.06; r=apr/12

M0=180e3   #starting balance
P=1000     #monthly repayment

M=M0       #initialize
T=0        #total interest paid
for n in range(6):
  I=M*r    #calculate the interest
  T=T+I    #add to running total
  X = [n,M,M0-M,I,T] #values to print
  print([round(num,2) for num in X])
  M=M+I-P  #apply the interest and payment

[0, 180000.0, 0.0, 900.0, 900.0]
[1, 179900.0, 100.0, 899.5, 1799.5]
[2, 179799.5, 200.5, 899.0, 2698.5]
[3, 179698.5, 301.5, 898.49, 3596.99]
[4, 179596.99, 403.01, 897.98, 4494.97]
[5, 179494.97, 505.03, 897.47, 5392.45]

The total amount of interest paid can also be found by subtracting the total amount of loan repaid from the sum of the payments.

Question 2 (a)#

In this question, we need to remember that the interest is compounded monthly at 6% and so 15 years is equivalent to 180 months:

# The first 15 years
M=M0
for n in range(15*12):
  M=M*(1+r)-P
print("After 15 years: {:.0%}".format((M0-M)/M0))

# The next 15 years
for n in range(15*12):
  M=M*(1+r)-P
print("After 30 years: {:.0%}".format((M0-M)/M0))

After 15 years: 16%
After 30 years: 56%

Question 2 (b)#

To determine the number of months required to finish the repayment scheme, we can either use trial and error or implement a break condition to let python do the checking for us:

M=M0
for n in range(1000):   #a big number keeps the loop going until the break
  M=M*(1+r)-P
  if M<0:
    break

print("To repay the total amount takes ",n,"months (",n//12, "years)")
print("Total interest:",P*n-M0)

To repay the total amount takes  461 months ( 38 years)
Total interest: 281000.0

Combining this answer with the result from part (a), we see that in the first 15 years only 16% of the balance is repaid, whilst 44% of the loan balance is repaid in the last eight years. The total repayment is more than 2.5 times the size of the loan!

Question 3#

Taking \(M=0\) in the formula and rearranging for \(P\) gives

\[\begin{equation*} P=M_0R^n/f, \quad f=\frac{R^n-1}{R-1} \end{equation*}\]

Therefore the result is calculated as shown:

R=1+r; n=30*12 #30 years

# When M=0
f=(R**n-1)/(R-1)
P=M0*R**n/f
print("Monthly repayments:",P)

#interest repaid
print("Total interest:",P*n-M0)

Monthly repayments: 1079.1909452749394
Total interest: 208508.74029897817

By paying an extra £80 per month the loan is repaid 8 years faster and with around £73,000 less interest. If the repayments were doubled to £2000 then the loan would be repaid in 10 years with an interest saving of £220,000!
