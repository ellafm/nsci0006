Customer loan#

A customer takes out a £180,000 loan at 6% APR with a repayment agreement of £1000 per month. The interest is applied at the end of each month immediately before payment is taken. The monthly interest rate is given by

\[\begin{equation*} r = \text{APR}/12 = 0.06/12=0.005. \end{equation*}\]

The interest is a constant fraction of the balance, so the amount of interest paid each month decreases as the balance is paid off. At the beginning of the loan term, almost all of the monthly repayment is used to cover the interest. The loan balance will decrease faster towards the end of the loan term.

We can carry out the calculation in Python using a loop:

apr=0.06; r=apr/12

M0=180e3   #starting balance
P=1000     #monthly repayment

M=M0       #initialize
for n in range(6):
  I=M*r    #calculate the interest
  X = [n,M,M0-M,I] #values to print
  print([round(num,2) for num in X])
  M=M+I-P  #apply the interest and payment

The penultimate line in the above code rounds each value in the list to 2 decimal places, using a technique called list comprehension. We have not introduced this technique and you do not need to understand how it works just yet.

Question 1#

Edit the above code so that each printed line includes the total amount of interest repaid, shown in the last column of the table.

Question 2#

(a) Calculate the amount of loan repaid after the first 15 years, and the amount repaid after the next 15 years. Give your answers as a percentage of the loan that has been repaid.

(b) Determine how long it takes to repay the full loan amount, and calculate the total amount of interest paid.

Question 3#

It can be shown mathematically that the amount owed in month \(n\) is given by the following formula:

\[\begin{equation*} M = M_0 R^n - P\left(\frac{R^n-1}{R-1}\right), \qquad R=(1+r). \end{equation*}\]

Use this formula to calculate the monthly repayment required to repay the loan in 30 years. How much interest will be paid in this case?
